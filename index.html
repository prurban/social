How this app works

Planner — bank every content idea, tap the status chip to walk it from Idea → Drafted → Scheduled → Posted. Marking it Posted moves it into the Posts database automatically (one entry per platform).

Posts — your permanent archive of everything published. A few days after posting, come back and type in the likes/comments/views so you learn what works. Search it before making new content — your best old posts are your best future posts.

Numbers — a once-a-week, two-minute check-in of followers and views per platform, with growth shown automatically.

Why manual? Facebook, Instagram and especially TikTok don't allow outside apps to reliably pull your organic content — every "connected" tool breaks when they change the rules. Two minutes a week of typing keeps this app working forever, and the weekly ritual itself keeps you across your numbers. The posting playbook lives in the PR Urban Brain app.

How this app works, in one breath: ideas go into the Planner, get posted to Facebook/Instagram/TikTok by a human, get marked Posted (which files them into the Posts archive), and once a week you log your Numbers. Over time the app becomes your memory of what works — and the proof your following is growing.
Planner — banking and scheduling content
What: every content idea lives here with a date, a type (Reel, Photo, Story, Carousel, Live) and platform tags — Facebook blue, Instagram pink, TikTok teal. Ideas are grouped by week so you can see the fortnight ahead at a glance.

How: fill in the "Plan a post" box — tap the platform chips to toggle them (one post can target all three) — and Add to plan. The ✎ edits the caption, ✕ tapped twice deletes.

The status chip is the workflow. Tap it to cycle: Idea (just a thought) → Drafted (filmed/written) → Scheduled (queued in the platform) → Posted (it's live). The moment you hit Posted, the app automatically files one entry per platform into the Posts archive — no double typing.

When: batch it — one planning session a week (the weekly routine in the PR Urban Brain's Marketing section pairs with this perfectly).

Why: consistency beats brilliance. A visible plan is the difference between "we post most weeks" and actually posting every week.
Posts — your archive & results database
What: every published post, forever — searchable, filterable by platform.

How: posts arrive here automatically when marked Posted in the Planner. 2–3 days after posting, open each platform's insights and type the likes, comments and views/reach into the boxes — they save the moment you tap away. ✕ twice removes an entry.

When: filling numbers takes a minute; do it with your weekly check-in.

Why: this is the "database" part — after a term you can search "trial" or "concert" and instantly see which posts actually pulled views and enrolments. Your best old posts are the recipe for your best future posts.
Numbers — the weekly growth check-in
What: one row per week per platform: total followers and the week's views/reach.

How: once a week (Sunday works), open FB/IG/TikTok, read the two numbers from each profile/insights page, type them in, Save this week. Saving again the same week just updates it. From week two onward the app shows growth automatically — green +up, red −down.

Why: platforms show you today's numbers; they're terrible at showing the trend. Six months of weekly rows tells you exactly which platform is worth your energy — and watching the green +numbers is genuinely motivating.
Why isn't this connected to the platforms automatically?
Because it can't be, reliably — and honesty beats a broken promise. TikTok doesn't allow outside tools to touch organic content at all, and Facebook/Instagram connections break whenever Meta changes its rules (usually at the worst moment). Tools that claim otherwise are ad-analytics products or paid schedulers with the same fragility.

This app trades ~2 minutes a week of typing for something that works forever, costs nothing and can't be taken away. For scheduling FB+IG posts in advance, Meta Business Suite (free, from Meta itself) is the safe official tool — plan here, schedule there, mark Posted here.
